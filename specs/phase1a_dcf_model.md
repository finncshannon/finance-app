## Module Context
The Value Model Builder is the core analytical engine of the app. This document
specifies the DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) model in full detail. Other valuation
models (DDM, Comps, Revenue-Based, LBO) will be specified in their own phase

## DCF Model — Architecture Overview

### Design Standard
Institutional grade. This should look and function like something a Wall Street
investment banker would use at their home desk. Every design decision is measured
against this standard.

### Sub-Tab Structure (Bloomberg FA-style)
Within the Model Builder module, when DCF is selected:

`Overview` | `Historical Data` | `Assumptions` | `DCF Model` | `Sensitivity` | `History`

Each sub-tab is a distinct view. Navigation between sub-tabs is smooth and instant.

---

## Ticker Entry & Model Selection Flow

### Entry Points
1. **Direct search:** Prominent search bar in Model Builder. Type a ticker.
2. **Cross-module context menu:** Right-click any ticker anywhere in the app
   (Scanner, Portfolio, Watchlist, Dashboard) → "Open in Model Builder" →
   smooth animated transition.

### The Search & Selection Panel
1. User types ticker in search bar
2. **Half-window panel expands** (like Spotlight on Mac / Bloomberg command line):
   - Ticker and company name
   - Auto-detection results (recommended model, confidence %, full reasoning)
   - Quick financial snapshot (key metrics used by detector)
   - Model selection: accept recommendation or override to different model
3. **Background pre-fetch:** While user reviews the recommendation panel, the app
   loads all financial data in the background. By the time user confirms, data is ready.
4. **Panel collapses** into two persistent header lines:
   - **Line 1 (Ticker Info Bar):** Ticker, company name, current price, daily change
     ($, %), market cap, sector. Bloomberg-style ticker strip.
   - **Line 2 (Model Info Bar):** Selected model (DCF), auto-detection confidence,
     other model-relevant context as needed.
5. Both lines are always visible while working. Click either to re-expand the
   full search/selection panel (change ticker or switch models).

### Auto-Detection Integration
- ModelDetector v4.0 runs automatically on every new ticker
- Shows full reasoning before applying: "Recommended: DCF. Confidence: 82%.
  Reasoning: Strong FCF margin (18%), 4 consecutive years positive FCF,
  sector preference for DCF."
- User reviews, then accepts or overrides
- All applicable models remain accessible regardless of recommendation

## Sub-Tab 1: Historical Data

### Purpose
Display the raw financial data pulled from Yahoo Finance. This is the "what happened"
foundation. Model-specific extracts — the DCF pulls what it needs, not full financial
statements. (Full statements live in the Research module.)

### Data Extracted for DCF (per year, up to 10 years)

**Income Statement:**
- Revenue
- Cost of Revenue / COGS
- → Gross Profit (derived)
- → Gross Margin (derived)
- Operating Expenses (SG&A)
- R&D (if material)
- Depreciation & Amortization
- → EBIT / Operating Income (derived)
- → Operating Margin (derived)
- Interest Expense
- Tax Rate (effective)
- Net Income
- EPS

**Balance Sheet:**
- Total Assets
- Current Assets
- Total Liabilities
- Current Liabilities
- → Working Capital (derived)
- Total Debt
- Net Debt
- Cash & Equivalents
- Total Equity

**Cash Flow:**
- Operating Cash Flow
- Capital Expenditures
- → Free Cash Flow (derived: OCF - CapEx)
- Dividends Paid

**Market Data:**
- Shares Outstanding
- Market Cap
- Beta
- Current Price

### Display Format
- Left-to-right chronological (oldest year left, most recent right)
- Raw numbers grouped with derived metrics in consistent rhythm:
  Revenue → Growth Rate (row gap) → EBITDA → EBITDA Margin
  (mirrors current Excel layout pattern)
- Growth rates shown inline with their base metric
- Up to 10 years historical (graceful degradation if fewer available)

### Data Sources
- Primary: Yahoo Finance (yfinance)
- Secondary: SEC EDGAR XBRL (already integrated in Screening Tool)
- Open question for Architect: evaluate combining sources to maximize historical depth

## Sub-Tab 2: Assumptions

### Purpose
All forward-looking assumptions that drive the DCF projection. Generated by the
assumption engine, fully overridable by the user. Separate from historical data.

### Assumption Engine Principles (Restated for DCF Context)
1. **Data-informed:** Engine analyzes historical financials to generate assumptions
2. **Transparent reasoning:** Every assumption has an audit trail explaining why
3. **Analyst consensus as reference only:** Shown alongside, not used as input
4. **Full manual override:** Every value editable, visual indicator for user-modified values
5. **Per-company calibration:** Assumptions reflect THIS company's profile, not generic defaults

### DCF-Specific Assumptions

**Revenue Assumptions:**
- Revenue growth rate per year (can curve: higher near-term, decelerating to terminal)
- Engine reasoning: based on historical CAGR, trend direction, acceleration/deceleration,
  industry median, company maturity stage

**Cost Structure Assumptions (each projected independently):**
- COGS as % of revenue (or absolute growth rate)
- SG&A as % of revenue
- R&D as % of revenue (if applicable)
- D&A trajectory (linked to CapEx schedule)
- Engine reasoning for each: based on historical margins, trend stability, sector benchmarks

**CapEx Assumptions:**
- CapEx as % of revenue per year
- Engine reasoning: historical CapEx intensity, sector median, growth phase of company

**Working Capital Assumptions:**
- NWC as % of revenue (derived from historical pattern)
- Change in NWC per year feeds into FCF calculation

**Tax Assumptions:**
- Effective tax rate (from historical, adjustable)

**WACC Components (CRITICAL — Primary Scenario Differentiator):**
- Risk-Free Rate: **auto-populated from live 10-year Treasury yield**
- Beta: auto-populated from Yahoo Finance, overridable
- Equity Risk Premium (ERP): **manually set by user** (engine provides default suggestion, e.g., 5.5%)
- Cost of Debt: derived from Interest Expense / Total Debt, overridable
- Tax Rate: derived from effective rate, overridable
- Capital Structure: derived from Market Cap vs. Total Debt
- → Cost of Equity = Risk-Free Rate + Beta × ERP
- → WACC = (E/V × Cost of Equity) + (D/V × Cost of Debt × (1 - Tax))
- **Every component visible and individually overridable**
- **Static across all 10 projection years** (locked decision)
- WACC is the primary differentiator between scenarios in investment banking —
  the assumption engine must treat it with the same depth and rigor as revenue growth

**Debt Schedule Assumptions:**
- Starting total debt (from most recent balance sheet)
- Repayment trajectory: simple assumption (X% retired per year based on FCF coverage)
- New issuance assumptions (default: none, overridable)
- → Projects net debt position per year
- → Derives interest expense per year (avg debt balance × cost of debt)
- → Feeds into FCF calculation and equity bridge
- Data limitation: detailed maturity schedules require Bloomberg or 10-K footnote parsing.
  MVP uses estimated repayment rate from historical patterns. Detailed maturity parsing
  flagged as future enhancement (Research module could surface this data).

**Terminal Value Assumptions:**
- Terminal growth rate (for perpetuity growth method)
- Exit EV/EBITDA multiple (for exit multiple method)
- Both always calculated and shown side-by-side

### Dynamic Scenario Generation
The assumption engine generates scenarios dynamically based on uncertainty level:
- **Low uncertainty** (stable company): 3 scenarios, tight spreads
- **Medium uncertainty** (established growth): 4 scenarios, moderate spreads
- **High uncertainty** (pre-revenue, turnaround): 5 scenarios, wide spreads

Uncertainty assessed from: historical metric volatility, analyst estimate range width,
company maturity stage, sector cyclicality, recent trend stability.

Each scenario varies assumptions (primarily WACC and growth rates) within
data-informed ranges. Scenarios are named contextually (Bear/Base/Bull/Deep Bear/
Strong Bull depending on count).

**Market-Implied Scenario:** Always generated. Uses MarketImpliedCalculator logic
to reverse-solve what the market is pricing in. Shown as reference alongside
engine-generated scenarios.

### Analyst Consensus Display
Shown as a reference column alongside engine-generated assumptions:
- "Engine base case revenue growth: 12%. Analyst consensus: 14%. Delta: -2pp."
- Does NOT feed into calculations
- Helps user calibrate whether their assumptions are aggressive or conservative
  relative to the street

## Sub-Tab 3: DCF Model (Projection & Output)

### Purpose
The actual 10-year projection of cash flows, discounting, terminal value, and
intrinsic value output. Reads from assumptions, not directly from historical data.

### Projection Table — Full Line-Item Build (Institutional Standard)

10 columns (Year 1 through Year 10), every line item projected independently:

```
REVENUE BUILD
  Revenue
  Revenue Growth Rate
  
COST STRUCTURE
  Cost of Revenue / COGS
  → Gross Profit
  → Gross Margin %
  SG&A / Operating Expenses
  R&D (if applicable)
  Depreciation & Amortization
  → EBIT / Operating Income
  → Operating Margin %

BELOW THE LINE
  Interest Expense (from debt schedule)
  Taxes (EBIT × Tax Rate)
  → NOPAT

FREE CASH FLOW BUILD
  NOPAT
  + D&A (add back non-cash)
  - CapEx
  - Change in Working Capital
  → Unlevered Free Cash Flow

DISCOUNTING
  WACC (static, from assumptions)
  Discount Factor
  → PV of FCF (per year)

TERMINAL VALUE (Year 10)
  Method 1: Perpetuity Growth
    FCF₁₀ × (1 + g) / (WACC - g)
  Method 2: Exit Multiple
    EBITDA₁₀ × Exit EV/EBITDA Multiple
  → PV of Terminal Value (both methods)
  → Terminal Value as % of Total EV (both methods)
  → Delta between methods highlighted

EQUITY BRIDGE
  Sum of PV of FCFs (Years 1-10)
  + PV of Terminal Value
  → Enterprise Value
  - Net Debt (from debt schedule, terminal year)
  + Cash & Equivalents
  → Equity Value
  ÷ Shares Outstanding
  → INTRINSIC VALUE PER SHARE
```

### Output Visualization
**Primary:** Valuation bridge / waterfall chart showing:
PV of Cash Flows → + Terminal Value → = Enterprise Value → - Net Debt → = Equity Value → ÷ Shares → Per Share Value

Clean, visual decomposition of where the value comes from.

**Terminal Value Comparison:**
Both perpetuity growth and exit multiple methods shown side-by-side.
Delta between them highlighted. Helps user assess model sensitivity to
terminal value methodology.

## Sub-Tab 4: Sensitivity (Sub-Module)

### Elevated to Core Feature
Dedicated tabbed section with four independent analytical tools:

**Within Sensitivity:**
`Sliders` | `Tornado` | `Monte Carlo` | `Tables`

### Sliders Tab
- Draggable sliders for each key assumption
  (revenue growth, WACC, terminal growth, margins, exit multiple, CapEx %)
- Intrinsic value updates in real-time as sliders move
- Multiple sliders adjustable simultaneously (compounding effects visible)
- Chart updates live alongside slider movement

### Tornado Tab
- Bars showing impact of ±1 standard deviation change per assumption
- Sorted by magnitude (biggest mover at top)
- Click any bar to drill into that variable's full range
- Immediately answers: "what assumption matters most for this company?"

### Monte Carlo Tab
- Core feature — build it right (Finn's directive)
- 10,000+ simulations varying all assumptions within calibrated ranges
- Ranges derived from assumption engine's uncertainty assessment
- User can adjust distribution parameters before running
- Output: probability distribution curve of intrinsic value
- Key stats: median, 10th/90th percentile, probability of upside vs. current price
- Current market price marked as reference line on distribution

### Tables Tab
- Classic 2-variable sensitivity grid
- User selects which two variables to cross (e.g., WACC vs. terminal growth)
- Color-coded: green = upside, red = downside vs. current price
- Raw numbers for users who want the data

## Sub-Tab 5: History (Version Tracking)

### Model Saving
- Every model run auto-saved per ticker
- Full state: all assumptions (engine + overrides), outputs, sensitivity configs, reasoning
- Reopening a ticker loads last saved state

### Version History
- Each save = versioned snapshot
- Browse: "AAPL — v3 (Jan 15) → v4 (Feb 1) → v5 (Feb 22)"
- Diff view between any two versions: what changed, how outputs shifted
- Timestamps + optional user annotations ("Updated after Q4 earnings")

### Export
- **Excel (.xlsx):** Formatted workbook — inputs, projections, scenarios, sensitivity tables.
  Readable standalone without the app.
- **PDF:** One-page professional valuation summary. Key outputs, scenario summary,
  waterfall chart, recommendation. Suitable for printing or sharing.

## Edge Cases & Data Gaps

### Insufficient Historical Data
- If <3 years of data available: show warning, allow model to run with available data
- Assumption engine adjusts: wider uncertainty bands, fewer scenarios
- Missing individual line items: engine uses available proxies or flags as "manual input required"

### Auto-Detection Low Confidence
- If ModelDetector confidence <60%: prominent warning on recommendation panel
- Show alternative model suggestions with their scores
- User must explicitly acknowledge before proceeding

### Companies Where DCF Doesn't Fit
- Pre-revenue companies: DCF technically runs but results are unreliable
- Engine should flag: "This company has no positive FCF history. DCF results
  have high uncertainty. Consider Revenue-Based model."
- Model still runs if user wants it — no hard blocks, only informed warnings

## Handoff Notes for PM Agent
- This is the most complex single component in the entire application
- The full line-item projection build is a hard requirement (not margin-driven shortcut)
- WACC is the primary scenario differentiator — treat its implementation with maximum rigor
- Debt schedule is included but MVP uses estimated repayment (not detailed maturities)
- The assumption engine's per-company calibration is what makes this institutional grade —
  do not allow simplification to generic defaults during implementation
- Sensitivity sub-module (especially Monte Carlo) is core, not nice-to-have
- All four sensitivity tools must be independently robust
- Terminal value: always show both methods with delta — this is a key analytical feature
- The waterfall visualization is the primary output view — invest in making it excellent
